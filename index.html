How We Do It

Our Techniques

We apply a wide range of concrete methods — from classic broom finishes to hand-crafted countertops. Here's a look at the techniques that define our work.

🪨 Stamped Concrete

A pattern or texture is pressed into fresh concrete to resemble stone, slate, flagstone, brick, tile, or wood. Stamped concrete is popular because it replicates premium materials at a lower cost while offering more color control and greater durability.

🎨 Staining

Either an acid or water-based stain is applied to color a concrete surface, giving it a rich, marbled look. Each installation is unique because the stain reacts naturally with the concrete.

🧱 Form Liners

A mold is used to cast a textured design or pattern directly into concrete walls or surfaces, enabling detailed architectural finishes and custom shapes that would be difficult to achieve any other way.

📐 Overlays & Toppings

A thin layer of polymer-modified concrete is applied over an existing floor or wall. Overlays can be troweled smooth (microtoppings) or stamped — either way, they give a brand-new look at a fraction of the cost of full replacement.

✨ Exposed Aggregate

The top cream layer of the surface is removed to expose the underlying aggregate. The surface is retarded, then washed the next day, acid-washed, and finished with a glossy sealer to enhance the natural shine of the stone.

🧹 Broom Finish

The classic exterior finish. A broom is dragged across fresh concrete to create a slip-resistant texture. Applied early for a more aggressive grip or later for a subtler finish — a durable, time-tested choice for driveways and walkways.

🌈 Colored Concrete

Concrete can be colored three primary ways: integral color mixed into the pour, color hardeners broadcast onto the surface, or stains applied by hand or spray. Each method produces a distinct look and depth of color.

🏔️ Coloring Stamped Concrete

Multiple colors are applied to stamped concrete to mimic natural stone — a base color in the mix, secondary colors worked in with trowels, then an accent color sprayed on during stamping to fill the low spots and emphasize the pattern.

🍽️ Countertops & Sinks

Concrete countertops and sinks offer fully custom shapes, sizes, and colors for a one-of-a-kind look. Available as pre-cast (formed and finished in our shop, allowing high polish) or cast-in-place (poured on site, often cost-effective for large surfaces).

Our Story

A Family Legacy in Concrete

Asfeld Construction Incorporated was started in 1981 when Dennis Asfeld combined his knowledge of concrete and custom home building. His father Norb joined the company shortly after and served as a partner until retiring in 1998.

In the early 90s Dennis recognized poured walls as the future of concrete foundations — he was one of the first five contractors in the Twin Cities to adopt this technique. Now poured walls are the industry standard.

Before retiring, Norb spent his final years teaching his grandson Ben the art of concrete finishing. Ben went on to earn a degree in Construction Management from the University of Wisconsin–Stout, then trained in advanced techniques including decorative finishes, concrete countertops, and cast-in-place sinks.

Today Ben and Dennis are a team united by old-school craftsmanship and modern decorative expertise — with more than 50 years of combined experience.

Early 90s — ACI adopts poured wall foundation technique

2004 — Ben & Dennis expand into stamped decorative concrete

2006 — Ben joins full-time with advanced decorative training
